# Analysis Notes

## Business Question
Are youth support resources evenly distributed across districts in Hong Kong?

## Approach
- Combined public data sources into a structured dataset
- Focused on youth population and available community resources
- Aggregated data at district level
- Compared resource availability against population size

## Key Findings
- Districts with higher youth population do not always have proportionally more centres.
- Resource distribution appears uneven across districts.
- Some districts may face higher pressure on existing facilities.
- Community infrastructure does not always scale with population.

## Interpretation
This suggests potential inefficiencies in resource allocation. Even without exact volunteer data, youth centres and community facilities can act as a proxy for engagement capacity.

## Recommendations
- Reallocate or expand services in high-demand districts
- Improve planning based on population trends
- Use data-driven approaches for future community development

## Reflection
This project demonstrates how public data can be used to identify real-world social issues, even with limited or indirect indicators.
